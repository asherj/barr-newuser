Intro

We created this document to help the folks who come to the meetings, who just don’t know where to start. There’s a lot of info available, but it’s not easily digestible. So hopefully these pages may serve as a starting place on your journey. This guide is geared toward the new 3d printer owner, If you are interested in building one, come to the meetings, there are lots of folks there with experience, happy to share. In sections where there are large numbers of items We have tried to limit the choices to just a few that are more approachable. The rest will be in the Resources page. If you are more interested in 3D design, and would rather have someone else print things, ask around or look at the Service Bureau section.

  • Tweak it for your purposes (TinkerCAD)
  • Convert it to g-code (Slic3r)
  • Send it to the printer
  • Print

Sources of Models

There are a few different "types" of models, Some are intended as assets for games, or just for illustrative purposes. Some are scans of existing objects. Some are design models used in manufacturing. Models that are suitable for 3D printing need to have certain characteristics; They need to be in one piece (no floating parts), They need to be watertight (have a defined inside & outside) The walls of the model must not be too thin, For FDM printers unsupported overhangs shouldn't be more than 45 degrees. There is more info on what makes a good 3D model in the Design section.
